The Power of Eliciting Wider Perspectives

The Power of Eliciting Wider Perspectives

Before Cassi even turns to external data, it first strengthens the quality of your leadership’s thinking. Through structured elicitation, Cassi forces clarity:

  • ✅ What are you trying to achieve?
  • ✅ How will you measure success?
  • ✅ What are the most important factors influencing your outcome?

This process alone reduces ambiguity, eliminates guesswork, and forces strategic discipline. Cassi ensures that before seeking outside input, your leadership team has already dramatically improved its own decision-making.

Step 2: Bringing in the Outside View

Nobel Prize-winning psychologist Daniel Kahneman showed that the outside view is the key to reducing bias in decision-making. Cassi makes this effortless.

With a few clicks, you can tap into precisely the diversity you need—whether by profession, geography, cognitive background, or industry expertise. Run forecasting competitions or crowdsource fresh insights to challenge internal assumptions.

This step is critical in protecting against:

  • 🔴 HIPPO bias (Highest Paid Person’s Opinion).
  • 🔴 Confirmation bias (seeking only information that supports your views).
  • 🔴 Groupthink (risking blind spots in strategic planning).

By integrating both inside and outside perspectives, Cassi ensures your organization is more agile, more informed, and more prepared than the competition. It also gives you a principled, data-backed approach to demonstrate to investors and stakeholders that your strategy is both rigorous and resilient.
